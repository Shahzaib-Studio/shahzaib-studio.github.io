Who I Am

STORY FIRST.
THEN VISUALS.

I've spent years at the intersection of documentary storytelling and generative AI — a combination that's still pretty rare. In these years, I've led creative direction across a wide range of client projects, from fully AI-generated documentaries to productions with real talking heads and real footage.

What I do well is see the story before the visuals exist, then use tools like Seedance, Runway, Sora, Midjourney, Grok, and Google Flow to build it frame by frame. I also know when a cut isn't working and why — the post-production judgment that's hard to teach.

My background in English Literature isn't incidental. It's why I think about narrative before aesthetics, and why my work tends to feel intentional rather than algorithmic.

AI-Generated Documentary

The Aaron Hernandez Story — Fame, Trauma & The Life He Couldn't Escape

A fully AI-generated documentary dissecting the rise and fall of one of the NFL's most gifted — and troubled — athletes. From an abusive father to a hidden sexuality he could never confront, this film asks why a star in the making ended up in a jail cell.

Runway Gen-3MidjourneySoraDaVinci ResolvePremiere Pro

▶ Autoplaying muted — unmute anytime

The Brief

Tell the story of Aaron Hernandez — not just the crime, but the psychology behind it. A man shaped by trauma, suppressed identity, and a sports culture that rewarded performance over wellbeing.

What I Did

Wrote and structured the entire narrative from research to script. Generated every visual — game footage reconstructions, emotional flashbacks, environmental storytelling — using AI tools. Directed the pacing and tone through post-production to match the weight of the subject matter.

The Result

A documentary that feels like it was shot, not generated. Every frame serves the story — the visual language reflects Aaron's internal conflict, not just the external events.
